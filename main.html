Mahatma Gandhi

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born
  • 1883 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi Marries Kasturbai Makhanji Kapadia
  • 1885 - Mahatma Gandhi's Father Dies at 63
  • 1888 - Mahatma Gandhi travels to London to become a lawyer
  • 1891 - Mahatma Gandhi returns to India after completing his studies
  • 1893 - Mahatma Gandhi leaves to africa to fight a case
  • 1894 - Mahatma Gandhi Founds the Natal Indian Congress
  • 1899 - Mahatma Gandhi Organizes an Indian Ambulance Corps of 1100 Men During His Service in the Boer War
  • 1906 - Mahatma Gandhi Introduces Non-Violent Protest Philosophy of Satyagraha
  • 1908 - Mahatma Gandhi is Arrested for the First Time, for Refusing to Carry an Obligatory ID Card in South Africa
  • 1910 - Mahatma Gandhi Establishes the Tolstoy Farm, which Serves as a Base of Operations for His Activities in South Africa
  • 1931 - Mahatma Gandhi Begins "Great March" to Gain Indian Rights in South Africa
  • 1914 - Mahatma Gandhi Suspends South African Struggle After Winning Passage of the Indian Relief Act
  • 1915 - Mahatma Gandhi Receives Hero's Welcome Upon Returning to India from South Africa
  • 1917 - Mahatma Gandhi Goes to Champaran to Investigate Conditions of Local Farmers and establishes Sabarmati Ashram
  • 1918 - Mahatma Gandhi Begins Advocating on Behalf of Farmers in Kheda Subject to Oppressive Taxation During a Famine
  • 1919 - British Government Passes the Rowlatt Act in Colonial India, Indefinitely Extending the Use of 'Emergency Measures'
  • 1919 - Jallianwala Bagh Massacre
  • 1920 - Mahatma Gandhi Begins the Non-Cooperation Movement
  • 1922 - Nationalist Mob Sets Fire to a Police Station in Chauri Chaura Incident, Prompting Mahatma Gandhi to Call Off the Non-Cooperation Movement
  • 1922 - Mahatma Gandhi is Arrested by the British Government on Charges of Inciting Violence at Chauri Chaura
  • 1924 - Mahatma Gandhi is Released from Prison After Serving Only Two Years of a Six-Year Sentence Following Surgery for Appendicitis
  • 1925 - Mahatma Gandhi Founds the All-India Spinners' Association
  • 1927 - Mahatma Gandhi's 'The Story of My Experiments with Truth' is Published
  • 1930 - Mahatma Gandhi Embarks on the Salt Satyagraha
  • 1931 - Lord Irwin, Viceroy of India, Signs the Gandhi-Irwin Pact
  • 1934 - Mahatma Gandhi Announces Retirement from Politics to Focus on Village Economics
  • 1944 - Kasturba Gandhi Dies at Age 74
  • 1946 - The British Cabinet Mission Arrives in India
  • 1946 - Direct Action Day, Also Known as the "Great Calcutta Killing"
  • 1946 - Formation of the Interim Government of India
  • 1947 - United Kingdom Passes the Indian Independence Act 1947
  • 1947 - The Partition of India
  • 1948 - Mahatma Gandhi Announces Fast to End Hindu/Muslim Violence in Delhi
  • 1948 - Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is Assassinated by Nathuram Godse

"Non-violence is the greatest force at the disposal of mankind. It is mightier than the mightiest weapon of destruction devised by the ingenuity of man"

-- Mahatma Gandhi
